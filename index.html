How the process works

  • 1

    Apply for your benefit

    Apply at VA.gov or check Hazlewood eligibility at TVC.Texas.gov. You'll receive a Certificate of Eligibility (COE).

  • 2

    Register for classes

    Enroll in courses through MySam. Make sure your courses are on your approved degree plan — the VA won't pay for courses outside of it.

  • 3

    Submit your request in MySam — every semester

    Complete the semester Request Certification Form and upload any required documents. This must be done each term, even if you've done it before.

  • 4

    We certify and notify you

    Your School Certifying Official reviews and processes your request. If there's an issue, we'll reach out by email, text, or phone.

Federal VA Benefits

VA education benefits are earned through your military service. There are several types — each one works a little differently. Find your chapter below to see what it covers and how payments work.

First time? Apply for your VA education benefit at VA.gov/education ↗. Once you receive your Certificate of Eligibility (COE), submit it through your MySam account.

Which chapter do you have?

Each VA benefit is identified by a "chapter" number. You can find yours on your Certificate of Eligibility (COE), or by calling the VA at 1-888-442-4551.

Chapter Common Name VA Pays SHSU Tuition? VA Pays You Directly?
Ch. 33Post-9/11 GI Bill®YesYes (housing + books)
Ch. 31Veterans Readiness & Employment (VRE)YesYes
Ch. 30Montgomery GI Bill® (MGIB)NoYes — you pay SHSU
Ch. 35Dependents' Educational Assistance (DEA)NoYes — you pay SHSU
Ch. 1606Montgomery GI Bill® – Selected ReserveNoYes — you pay SHSU

Details by chapter

The most comprehensive VA education benefit. Best for veterans who served after September 10, 2001.

What it covers

  • Tuition and fees paid directly to SHSU, based on your entitlement percentage
  • Book stipend up to $1,000 per academic year — deposited to your bank account
  • Monthly Housing Allowance (MHA) based on your local zip code and how many credits you're taking

Housing allowance rules

  • You must be enrolled more than half time (above 51% rate of pursuit) to receive any MHA
  • If you're only taking online courses, you receive 50% of the national average MHA
  • The more credits you take, the higher your MHA (up to full time)
Full time for undergrads = 12+ credits for Fall/Spring. The VA calculates your MHA by dividing your enrolled hours by 12. Courses not on your degree plan are not counted.

Important to know

  • Hazlewood cannot be combined with Ch. 33 — unless your VA entitlement is below 100%
  • Academic suspension is reported to the VA and can affect your benefit

For veterans with a service-connected disability. Your VA Vocational Rehabilitation & Employment (VRE) Counselor manages everything.

How payments work

  • Your VRE counselor sends a Purchase Order (PO) to SHSU to cover tuition and fees
  • Undergraduates: The PO should include a line for required books — usable at Barnes & Noble or through the Bearkat Bundle
  • Graduate students: Your VRE counselor will issue a separate PO directly to Barnes & Noble for textbooks

Important to know

  • Contact your VRE counselor with any questions about MHA or coverage
  • Hazlewood cannot be combined with Chapter 31

The original Montgomery GI Bill for active duty members who enrolled and paid into the program. The VA sends payments to you — not to SHSU.

How payments work

  • The VA deposits payments directly into your bank account
  • You are responsible for paying SHSU's tuition and fees yourself

Enrollment rates

  • Full time: 12+ credits  |  ¾ time: 9–11  |  ½ time: 6–8
  • Less than ½ time: 4–5  |  ¼ time or less: 1–3
Hazlewood can be combined with Chapter 30.

For eligible dependents (spouses and children) of veterans who are permanently and totally disabled or who died in service.

How payments work

  • The VA deposits payments directly into the student's bank account
  • The student is responsible for paying SHSU tuition and fees
Hazlewood can be combined with Chapter 35.

Hazlewood Act

A Texas state benefit for veterans who entered military service as Texas residents. It provides up to 150 credit hours of tuition exemption at Texas public universities.

What does Hazlewood cover?

Hazlewood waives up to 90% of standard tuition and most fees.

Not covered: student service fees, athletic fees, housing, meals, books, supplies, late fees, health center fees, study abroad fees, and living expenses.

Which type applies to you?

The veteran uses their own 150-hour benefit to pay for their own education.

Documents you'll need

  • DD-214 (your military discharge document)
  • Completed Hazlewood Exemption Application
  • Proof of Texas residency
  • Certificate of Eligibility — showing no federal education benefits are available
  • Must meet SHSU's Financial Aid SAP (GPA) requirements

The veteran transfers some or all of their 150-hour benefit to a dependent — a spouse, biological child, stepchild, adopted child, or tax dependent.

The dependent must be under age 25 and both the veteran and dependent must be Texas residents.

Documents you'll need

  • DD-214
  • Completed Hazlewood Exemption Application
  • Proof of Texas residency for both the student and the veteran
  • Certificate of Eligibility — showing no federal education benefits are available
  • Proof of relationship (birth certificate, marriage license, adoption documents, or tax transcripts)
  • Must meet SHSU's Financial Aid SAP requirements
  • Selective Service registration (for male dependents)

For dependents of a veteran who is 100% permanently and totally disabled, missing in action (MIA), or killed in action (KIA). There is no hour transfer limit.

The student must be a Texas resident. The veteran does not need to be a Texas resident for this type.

Documents you'll need

  • DD-214
  • 100% Permanent & Total VA disability rating documentation
  • Completed Hazlewood Exemption Application
  • Certificate of Eligibility — showing no GI Bill® benefits available
  • Proof of relationship (birth certificate, marriage license, adoption documents, or tax transcripts)
  • No defaulted Texas guaranteed student loans
  • Must meet SHSU's Financial Aid SAP requirements
  • Selective Service registration (for male dependents)

Active Duty, Guard & Reserve

If you're currently serving, this page explains Tuition Assistance, what happens if you're deployed mid-semester, and your options for managing enrollment.

Before you enroll: If you plan to use Tuition Assistance, you must get approval from your Educational Services Officer (ESO) or Education Counselor at your base first. Do this before registering for classes.

Each military branch manages Tuition Assistance (TA) differently. Start with your branch's Education Counselor.

  • Air Force / Air Force Reserve / Air National Guard Apply via AFVEC ↗
  • Army / Army Reserve / Army National Guard Apply via ArmyIgnitED ↗
  • Navy Navy College ↗
  • Coast Guard Coast Guard TA ↗
  • Army National Guard NationalGuard.com ↗
Even if you're waiting on TA approval, you must still meet SHSU's payment deadline. See Student Account Services ↗ for due dates.
Important: Refunds are only available to Reservists and National Guard members called into active duty. They are not available if you voluntarily enlisted or if you're already active duty being deployed.

Options for Reservists and National Guard called to duty mid-semester

  • Refund — Get a full refund of tuition and fees for that semester
  • Incomplete grade (X) — Receive an "X" on your transcript; you have one semester to finish the work
  • Final grade — If enough work is done, your instructor may assign a final grade at their discretion

Resignation process

  • 1

    Submit the Resignation Form

    Complete the Resignation Form ↗ through the Registrar's Office.

  • 2

    Email the Registrar from your SHSU email

    Send to registrar@shsu.edu.
    Subject: "Resignation Additional Documents – Military Orders (Sam ID 000xxxxxx)"
    Attach your official military orders.

  • 3

    Wait for confirmation

    You'll receive a response within five business days.

If you're in the Army, Army Reserve, or Army National Guard, you apply for TA through ArmyIgnitED.

  • 1

    Select your Education Path in ArmyIgnitED

    Get approval from your Army Education Counselor. SHSU also needs to approve your path. You must already be accepted into the SHSU program you're requesting.

  • 2

    Register for classes in MySam

    Register before the first class day. See SHSU registration deadlines ↗.

  • 3

    Verify your courses appear in ArmyIgnitED

    They should show up within 7 business days. If not, email registrar@shsu.edu. Then request TA approval.

  • 4

    Meet the payment deadline

    You're still responsible for the payment deadline even while TA is pending. See payment due dates ↗.

Dropping a course (Q-Drop)

You can drop one course and stay enrolled in others using a Q-Drop after Census Day. But be aware:

  • Q-dropping can reduce your VA rate of pursuit, lowering your housing allowance
  • It could result in a debt owed to the VA
  • It may affect Hazlewood SAP eligibility
  • If you're dropping your only class, you need to submit a resignation instead
More on Q-Drops ↗

Requesting an Incomplete grade

If you're called to service mid-semester, you can ask each instructor for an Incomplete grade ("X").

  • Contact each instructor individually — each one decides on their own
  • If approved, you have one semester to complete the work
  • A grade of "X" will appear on your transcript until the work is finished

Payments & Housing

It depends on your chapter:
  • Ch. 33 (Post-9/11): Yes — SHSU receives payment directly based on your entitlement %
  • Ch. 31 (VRE): Yes — via Purchase Order from your VRE Counselor
  • Ch. 30, 35, 1606: No — the VA pays you; you are responsible for paying SHSU
If you have Chapter 33 (Post-9/11), yes — up to $1,000 per academic year for books and supplies. The amount is based on your enrollment and entitlement percentage. It's deposited to your bank account at the start of each term.
The more credits you're enrolled in, the higher your monthly housing allowance (MHA). If you drop below half time, you get no MHA at all. For Chapter 33, you must have a rate of pursuit above 51% to receive any housing payment. Students enrolled only in online classes receive half the national average MHA.
